Recommended Structure
admin.py — your main interface
Register everything meaningful here. Django Admin gives you listing + CRUD essentially for free. Focus customization on:

Reservation — list with date, slot, status, responsible member, invoice link
Contract — list with member, status, dates
Invoice — read-mostly ledger view, list with status, amounts, type
Workspace / WorkspaceSetup — inline setups under workspace
Member — inline emails, memberships

views.py — two custom pages beyond admin
Your professor wants two sites minimum. I'd suggest:
Site 1 — Reservation Form (/reserve/)
A clean public-facing or staff form to create a reservation. This is where forms.py earns its place — validate that the setup/date/slot combo isn't already taken, show available workspaces, etc. On valid submit, just call reservation.save() and let the trigger generate the invoice.
Site 2 — Reports / Dashboard (/reports/)
Surface your two SQL views here. Use raw SQL or unmanaged models to query view_revenue_by_client_type and center_occupancy. A simple template with two tables is enough — but it directly demonstrates your DB-first philosophy to the professor.
urls.py
Three entries: admin, /reserve/, /reports/.
